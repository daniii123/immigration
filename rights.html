Laws

Immigration laws determine who is allowed into the country and how long they can stay. The laws also control what they can do during their time in the country. Immigrants have their rights to be in the country however, many immigrants do not know their rights.

Amendments

The US constitutional laws are applied to undocumented immigrants. Immigrants have their rights. Many parts of the amendments states "people" or "person" instead of "citizen". The fifth amendment - The right to due process

  • Immigrants have the right to due process
  • Reno v. Flores (1993) separation of parent and children “expelled removal” created by Illegal Immigration Reform and Immigrant Responsibility Act of 1996
  • Immigrants who have been in the country illegally for more than two years and are apprehended within 100 miles of the border can be deported almost immediately without going through a court hearing. (assylum services are an exception)

The sixth amendment - The right to legal counsel: This connects to immigrants because a lot of the time when people dont speak english they are takem advantage of, sometimes immigrants need to have a hearing so the judges know exactly why theyre here.

  • All criminal cases the accused should have the right to defend themselves.
  • Gideon V. Wainwright says if a person is too poor to hire an attorney, the government must appoint one for them.
  • Because most deportation processes are civil rather than criminal cases, the right to legal counsel often does not apply.

The 14th and 15th amendment - The right to vote or hold office: A person who has become a U.S citizen has the right to vote and hold office.

  • Everybody has a right to an education
  • Even kids who are undocumented immigrants should be able to access public education (Plyer v. Doe)

4th amendment - right against unreasonable search and seizure: When ICE agents show up at an undocumented persons house, they have the right to not let them in without a warrant signed by a judge. With fear, people dont know what they're entitled to.
